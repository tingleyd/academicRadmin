AcademicRadmin

Introduction

Academic administration–e.g., managing the planning of courses, the advising of students, and the representation of data from learning technologies, is a data intensive process. To date, the vast majority of approaches to academic administration take one of two paths: a) large scale, closed source, system purchases from software vendors or b) low technology solutions that entail large amounts of staff, and faculty, time engaging in a manual processes.

AcademicRadmin is a foray into changing parts of the academic administration ecosystem using open source tools.

We are guided by several principles:

  1. We use open source tools to offer options, especially R (hence academicRadmin), but Python based approaches are also just as welcome. Its more about the Radmin than the R.
  2. We provide the code that runs anything we use. You will probably have to adapt it to your own institution/use case, but that is ok!
  3. We explicitly encourage and foster student and administrative staff–rather than just IT professionals and faculty–to contribute and help drive conversations.

In this website we collect a range of examples that cover use cases for different communities: students, teachers, departmental administrators, and beyond.

This project was initiated by Harvard’s Vice Provost for Advances in Learning Research Group. Many of the tools discussed are R Shiny applications deployed with the help of our partners at Harvard’s Institute for Quantitative Social Science.
